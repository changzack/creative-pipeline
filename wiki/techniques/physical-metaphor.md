# Technique: Physical Metaphor

**Status:** proven (highest-impact creative technique)

## Description
Designing the artifact as if it were a real physical object — a printed card, a coin, a tape reel, a blueprint, a seismograph readout. The metaphor drives every design decision: material, texture, typography, animation.

## Evidence
- Run: V1, "Countdown Tape", rated **acceptable** — tape reel metaphor with perforations
- Run: Quiz V3, "Pile Up", rated **acceptable** — physical stacking of cards
- Run: Quiz V3, "Bleed Through", rated **acceptable** — ink/print overprinting metaphor
- Run: Quiz V3, "Seismic", rated **acceptable** — seismograph/amplitude readout

**4 of 6 acceptable builds used physical metaphors.** This is the strongest signal in our data.

## Why It Works
- Gives the builder a consistent creative constraint (everything must reference the physical object)
- Prevents the "dark card + light text" default because the metaphor demands specific materials
- Creates texture and depth naturally (paper has grain, metal has sheen, tape has perforations)
- Makes the artifact memorable — "it's the one that looks like a coin" vs "it's the dark one with gold text"

## How to Deploy
The metaphor should appear in the designer's approach doc as the FIRST decision. Everything flows from it:
- **Material** → what CSS textures/surfaces to use
- **Typography** → what fonts/weights reference this object
- **Animation** → what physical process does the reveal simulate
- **Color** → what palette belongs to this material world
- **Layout** → how would this object be structured

## Anti-Pattern
"Inspired by luxury branding" is NOT a physical metaphor. It's a vibes statement. A physical metaphor is specific: "This card is a pressed vinyl record sleeve" or "This is a boxing weigh-in ticket."

## Convergence Risk
LLMs gravitate toward the same metaphors:
- ⚠️ "Premium metal card" → everyone picks gold on black
- ⚠️ "Vintage fight card" → BANNED (V3c convergence)
- ⚠️ "Magazine cover" → safe but unambitious

Push for unusual metaphors: seismograph, pressed flower, transit ticket, trading card hologram, concrete monument, neon sign, blueprint.
